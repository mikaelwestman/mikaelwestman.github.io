Why do designers love the flag of Japan? Why is there an AK47 in the Mozambique flag? And why do so many American states have terrible flags?

The subreddit r/vexillology and this 99% Invisible episode opened up the world of vexillology to me and inspired me to to take a stab at flag design.

I decided to create flags for fictional countries and regions using the 6 principles of flag design:

  1. Keep it simple
  2. Use meaningful symbolism
  3. Use two to three basic colors
  4. No lettering or seals of any kind
  5. Be distinctive

*But as we all know, rules are made to be broken.

Neo Hellas — A secular Greek state, removing the white cross of Eastern Orthodox Christianity and replacing it with 2 circles, representing the mainland and disputed island of Cyprus.

Cherry Valley of Nangijala — From the classic Astrid Lindgren story 'The Brothers Lionheart'. A heavenly valley of cherry blossom trees where people go after death.

Thorn Rose Valley of Nangijala — Also from 'The Brothers Lionheart'. A region suppressed by a tyrant named 'Tengil' and his army, which are identified by their iconic helmets.

UCA, The United Coasts of America — A republic formed by the wealthy costal elites of the USA. Motto: "Divided by land, unified in thought".

UFSA, The United Flyover States of America — The country passed over during transcontinental flights over America.
